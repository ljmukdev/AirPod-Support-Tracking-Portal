**Research Date:** January 2026

## Executive Summary

The Spanish eBay market for individual AirPods parts shows **similar demand patterns** to the UK, with active sold listings for left/right earbuds and charging cases across all generations. However, there are notable price differences and market considerations.

**Key Finding:** The Spanish market appears viable but with **10-20% lower selling prices** compared to the UK market for equivalent items.

## Market Observations

### Positive Indicators

1. **Active Market Exists**: Sold listings confirm buyers actively purchase individual parts
2. **Same Product Demand**: All generations you currently sell (Pro 1st/2nd, 3rd Gen, 2nd Gen) have buyers
3. **Model Numbers Match**: Spanish buyers use same A-codes (A2698, A2699, A2700, etc.)
4. **Replacement Market**: Clear demand for single earbuds to replace lost/damaged ones
5. **Pro 2nd Gen USB-C Premium**: Newer USB-C models command similar or higher prices

### Challenges

1. **Lower Average Prices**: Most items sell for 10-20% less than UK equivalents
2. **Currency Risk**: EUR/GBP fluctuations affect margins
3. **Shipping Costs**: International shipping from UK adds cost (unless sourcing locally in Spain)
4. **Platform Fees**: eBay.es may have different fee structures
5. **Competition**: Local Spanish sellers exist (e.g., listings from professional sellers)
6. **Language**: Listings need Spanish titles/descriptions for discoverability

## Operational Considerations

### Option A: Sell from UK to Spain

**Pros:**
- Use existing inventory and systems
- Maintain GBP accounting
- Single operation to manage

**Cons:**
- International shipping costs (~£5-10 to Spain)
- Longer delivery times (5-10 days)
- Returns more complex
- Less competitive on price

### Option B: Source & Sell Locally in Spain

**Pros:**
- Local shipping (cheaper, faster)
- No currency conversion on purchases
- Competitive with local sellers
- Same-country returns

**Cons:**
- Need Spanish sourcing (eBay.es, Wallapop, etc.)
- Separate inventory management
- Potential tax/business registration requirements
- Language barrier for customer service

### Option C: Hybrid Approach

- Test market with UK inventory first
- Identify best-selling generations in Spain
- Gradually source locally for high-volume items
- Maintain UK operation as primary

## Conclusion

**Viability: MODERATE - Proceed with Caution**

The Spanish eBay market for AirPods parts is active and follows similar patterns to the UK. However, the 10-20% lower price points mean your profit margins will be compressed. The business model can work, particularly for AirPods Pro 2nd Gen and Pro 1st Gen, but AirPods 2nd Gen margins may not be sustainable.

A test phase with selective inventory is recommended before committing to full market entry.
